# Legal_Vault_Chapter.md: Contract Law Fundamentals

## 1. Concept Primer
*   **Offer:** A proposal initiated by a **promisor/offeror** to another person (the **promisee/offeree**) to perform or abstain from performing an act.
*   **Formation of Contract:** A valid contract requires **acceptance** of an offer, representing the offeree's consent to the terms.
*   **Consensus ad Idem:** The essential "meeting of the minds" required between parties for a contract to be legally binding.
*   **Void Contracts (Public Policy):** Agreements are void under public policy if they involve:
    1.  Inducing a public official to act in a specific manner.
    2.  Performance of an illegal act.
    3.  Collusive bidding regarding public contracts.

## 2. Landmark Facts & Applications
*   **Case Study A (General Offer):** A reward offer requires the claimant to have knowledge of the offer at the time of performance. If a person (e.g., Sunil) performs the act without knowing of the reward, there is no *consensus ad idem*, and they are not entitled to the reward.
*   **Case Study B (Business Contracts):** In "arms-length" transactions between business entities, the court does not invalidate contracts based on the value of consideration or profit margins (e.g., 84% vs 68%). Courts will not rewrite deals simply because one party secured a more favorable outcome, provided there is no evidence of fraud.

## 3. Trap Analysis
*   **The "Unfairness" Trap:** Students often mistakenly label unbalanced profits in B2B contracts as "unconscionable." If the parties are at "arms-length," high margins do not render a contract void.
*   **The "Performance without Knowledge" Trap:** Merely performing the requested act is insufficient; one must be aware of the offer to "accept" it.
*   **Scope of Public Policy:** Public policy is strictly limited to cases involving bribery of public officials, illegal acts, and collusion. It does not encompass bad commercial deals or greed.
