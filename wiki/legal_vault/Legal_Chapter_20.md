## 🏛️ Concept Primer: Important Commissions in India

Commissions and committees are appointed by the Government to investigate specific issues, recommend legal reforms, or oversee constitutional functions. In CLAT, questions often test the **purpose** and **significance** of these bodies.

### 1. Statutory and Constitutional Bodies
- **NITI Aayog**: The premier policy think tank of the Government of India, providing both directional and policy inputs. It replaced the Planning Commission.
- **National Human Rights Commission (NHRC)**: A statutory body responsible for the protection and promotion of human rights.
- **Law Commission of India**: A non-statutory body (usually headed by a retired Judge) that recommends legal reforms and reviews laws to ensure they are updated.

### 2. Historical Landmark Commissions
These are frequently tested in "Static GK" and "Legal Awareness" sections:

| Commission | Focus Area |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Sarkaria Commission** | Center-State Relations (recommendations on the role of Governor and Art. 356). |
| **Mandal Commission** | Reservations for Other Backward Classes (OBCs) in public employment. |
| **Nanavati Commission** | Inquiry into the 1984 anti-Sikh riots and the 2002 Godhra train burning. |
| **Srikrishna Committee** | Drafted the personal data protection framework and examined the Telangana statehood issue. |
| **Verma Committee** | Proposed amendments to criminal law following the 2012 Nirbhaya case (led to Criminal Law Amendment Act 2013). |

## 📖 Reading Passage: The Role of the Law Commission

*"The Law Commission of India plays a pivotal role in the evolution of the Indian legal system. By conducting research and reviewing existing laws, it identifies areas where legislation is obsolete or inconsistent with constitutional values. Its reports often form the basis for major legal amendments, such as the reforms in the Law of Contracts and the inclusion of digital evidence in the Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam."*

### 🔍 Trap Analysis
- **Trap**: "The Law Commission's recommendations are binding on the Government."
- **Fact**: They are **advisory** only. The Government is not legally bound to implement them.
