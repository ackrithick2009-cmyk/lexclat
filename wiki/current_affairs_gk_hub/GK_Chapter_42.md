# Chapter 42: Geography (Strategic Foundation)

Geography is a critical pillar for CLAT and other law entrance exams, providing the spatial context for international relations, environmental laws, and national disputes. This module focuses on Mathematical, Physical, and Cartographic fundamentals.

---

## 1. Branches of Geography
Modern geography is categorized into three primary domains:
*   **Mathematical Geography:** Focuses on the Earth's shape, rotational/recreational mechanics, and the calculation of time zones.
*   **Cartography:** The science of map-making, specifically the representation of topographical and physical features.
*   **Physical Geography:** The study of natural systems, including landforms, water bodies, and climate patterns.

## 3. Global Landforms & Continents
### Asia
The largest continent, home to extreme geological features:
*   **Water Bodies:** Caspian Sea, Aral Sea, Lake Baikal.
*   **Landforms:** The Himalayas (Highest Peak: Mt. Everest, 8,848m), Gobi Desert.

### Africa
Known for its massive river systems and arid landscapes:
*   **Water Bodies:** Lake Victoria, Lake Tanganyika, Nile River (World's longest).
*   **Landforms:** Atlas Mountains, Sahara Desert, Kalahari Desert.

## 4. Indian Physical Geography
### The Himalayan Range
The Himalayas are divided into several distinct regions, including the **Assam** and **Central** Himalayas.
*   **Highest Peaks:** Mt. Everest (8,848m), Kanchenjunga (8,598m).
*   **Glaciers:** Siachen (75 km - Strategic Importance), Hispar (62 km), Baltoro (58 km).

### Mountain Passes
Strategic gateways across the mountains:
*   **Banihal Pass:** Connects Jammu and Srinagar.
*   **Shipki La:** Connects Himachal Pradesh and Tibet.
*   **Rohtang Pass:** Gateway to Lahaul and Spiti.
