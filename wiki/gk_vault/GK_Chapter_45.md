# GK_Vault_Chapter.md: Medieval & Colonial History

## I. Concept Primer
This module explores the evolutionary arc of Indian history, spanning the Sultanate era's administrative experiments, the architectural and territorial consolidation of the Mughal Empire, and the transition of British governance from commercial enterprise to formal administrative statehood.

*   **Sultanate Era:** Characterized by centralized authority and economic interventions, most notably price control mechanisms and currency reform.
*   **Mughal Ascendancy:** A period of military expansion and state-building marked by significant battlefield engagements and the implementation of the Mansabdari system.
*   **British Consolidation:** The shift in British focus from mercantile trade to systemic legal and land-tenure reforms (e.g., Cornwallis’ administrative restructuring).

## III. Trap Analysis (Common CLAT Pitfalls)
*   **The "Unattributed" Trap:** Do not conflate Akbar’s religious syncretism (abolition of Jizya) with forced conversion.
*   **The "False Identity" Trap:** While Muhammad-bin-Tughlaq is labeled the "wisest fool," avoid assigning inaccurate historical myths to his reign unless explicitly supported by the text.
*   **The "Chronological Overlap" Trap:** Distinguish clearly between Plassey (1757) and Buxar (1764) regarding British influence.
*   **The "False Author" Trap:** Do not attribute the Pitt’s India Act (1784) to Lord Cornwallis; he assumed office in 1786, two years after the Act's passage.
