## 🏛️ Concept Primer: Global Literature & Notable Authors

Literature is a frequent topic in the General Awareness section of CLAT and AILET. Questions often focus on **Nobel Laureates**, **Man Booker Prize** winners, and **classic literary works**.

### 1. Global Literary Giants
- **Salman Rushdie**: An Indian-born British-American novelist. Key works include:
    - *Midnight's Children* (Booker of Bookers).
    - *The Ground Beneath Her Feet*.
    - *The Satanic Verses*.
- **Jonathan Swift**: An Anglo-Irish satirist known for *Gulliver's Travels*.
- **Fyodor Dostoevsky**: A Russian novelist who explored the human psyche in works like *Crime and Punishment* and *The Brothers Karamazov*.

### 2. Literary Awards
- **Nobel Prize in Literature**: Awarded annually since 1901 to an author from any country who has, in the words of the will of Alfred Nobel, produced "in the field of literature the most outstanding work in an ideal direction."
- **Man Booker Prize**: Awarded annually for the best original novel written in the English language and published in the UK.

## 🎭 Indian Cinema and Arts
- **Biopics**: Indian cinema frequently produces biographical films about national leaders, such as the film *Indira Gandhi*, which reflect the intersection of arts and political history.

---

### 🔍 Trap Analysis
- **Trap**: "The Nobel Prize in Literature is awarded for a specific book."
- **Fact**: It is usually awarded for an author's **entire body of work**, although specific works are often highlighted in the citation.
- **Trap**: "Only British authors can win the Man Booker Prize."
- **Fact**: Since 2014, the prize has been open to any novel written in English and published in the UK, regardless of the author's nationality.
