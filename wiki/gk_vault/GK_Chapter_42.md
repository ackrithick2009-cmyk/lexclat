# GK_Vault_Chapter.md: Vocabulary Proficiency (Set 42)

## 1. Concept Primer
In CLAT and LL.B. entrance examinations, vocabulary questions test the candidate's ability to identify **synonyms** (words with similar meanings) or **antonyms** (words with opposite meanings). 

*   **Methodology:** Analyze the root of the word and the context in which it typically appears.
*   **Strategy:** Eliminate distractors first. In legal contexts, identify role-based pairs (e.g., Plaintiff vs. Defendant).

## 2. Landmark Vocabulary Facts

| Word | Context | Primary Definition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Defendant** | Legal | An individual/entity accused or sued in a court of law. |
| **Inevitably** | Logical | Something that is certain to happen; unavoidable. |
| **Massive** | Descriptive | Exceptionally large, bulky, or heavy. |
| **Ceased** | Temporal | Brought to an end or stopped. |
| **Untenable** | Argumentative | Not able to be maintained or defended against attack or objection. |
| **Empty** | Physical/Abstract | Containing nothing; void. |

## 3. Trap Analysis (The "LexCLAT" Logic)

*   **Defendant:** Avoid choosing "claimant." The "Plaintiff" is the correct legal antonym.
*   **Inevitably:** Do not confuse with "Unexpectedly." The synonym is "Unavoidably."
*   **Massive:** Be cautious of antonyms like "Meager." Verify if the question seeks a synonym or antonym.
*   **Untenable:** "Untenable" signifies a weak position. "Viable" serves as the logical antonym.
*   **Empty:** "Full" is the direct antonym; do not overthink simple descriptors.
