# GK Vault Chapter 48: Modern & Medieval Indian History

## I. Concept Primer
*   **Social & Religious Reform Movements:**
    *   **Young Bengal Movement:** Established by Henry Vivian Louis Derozio.
    *   **Philosophy:** Shri Narayan Guru is credited with the famous aphorism: "No religion, no caste, no God for mankind."
*   **The Struggle for Independence:**
    *   **Frontier Gandhi:** The specific title attributed to Khan Abdul Gaffar Khan.
    *   **Khilafat Movement:** Its collapse was largely triggered by the political secularization and changes in Turkey, specifically the accession of Kamal Pasha.
    *   **Gandhi-Irwin Pact:** A historic agreement that included the withdrawal of ordinances, the release of political prisoners, and the authorization of peaceful picketing of liquor and opium shops.
*   **Mughal & Medieval Eras:**
    *   **Third Battle of Panipat (1761):** Fought between the Maratha Empire and Ahmed Shah Abdali.
    *   **Battle of Haldighat (1576):** A clash between Mughal forces, commanded by Man Singh, and Rajput forces led by Maharana Pratap.
    *   **Mughal Moniker:** Emperor Muhammad Shah earned the title 'Rangeela' due to his flamboyant and extravagant lifestyle.

## III. Trap Analysis (High-Yield Error Prevention)
*   **The "Lawyer" Categorization:** Many prominent leaders like Bal Gangadhar Tilak, Bhulabai Desai, and M.K. Gandhi were trained lawyers. Exercise caution when answering "not a lawyer" questions.
*   **Battle Commanders vs. Emperors:** In the Battle of Haldighat, the Mughal army was commanded by **Man Singh**, not Akbar, even though Akbar was the Emperor.
*   **The "Frontier" Names:** Do not confuse Khan Abdul Gaffar Khan (Frontier Gandhi) with other family members such as Wali Khan.
*   **Battle of Hydaspes:** Do not fall for the trap that lists Chandragupta Maurya as the opponent of Alexander. The correct opponent is **King Porus**.
