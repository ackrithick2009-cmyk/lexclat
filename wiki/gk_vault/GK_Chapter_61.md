# GK_Vault_Chapter.md: Sports Governance & Cricket World Cup History

## I. Concept Primer
*   **Governing Bodies:** International entities responsible for the standardization of rules, organization of tournaments, and oversight of professional conduct in sports.
*   **ICC Men’s Cricket World Cup:** A quadrennial international cricket tournament governed by the International Cricket Council (ICC).
*   **Hat-Trick (Cricket):** The act of taking three wickets in three consecutive legal deliveries during a match.
*   **Tournament Accolades:** 
    *   **Man of the Final:** Recognizes the most valuable player in the championship decider.
    *   **Man of the Tournament:** An award inaugurated in **1992** to recognize the best overall player in the competition.

## III. Trap Analysis
*   **2019 Result Logic:** England's victory in 2019 was decided by boundary count after a tie, not by a conventional run or wicket margin.
*   **The 1992 Constraint:** The "Man of the Tournament" award did not exist before 1992; avoid assuming its existence for 1975-1987 events.
*   **Venue vs. Host:** A tournament may have multiple hosts, but the final is tied to a single specific stadium (e.g., 2011 Final at Wankhede).
*   **Terminology:** Follow the provided text's nomenclature (e.g., IAAF for Athletics).
