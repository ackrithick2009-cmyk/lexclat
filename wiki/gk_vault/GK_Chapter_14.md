# GK_Vault_Chapter 14: Constitutional Foundations (Fundamental Duties)

### Concept Primer
*   **Definition:** Fundamental Duties were incorporated into the Indian Constitution via the 42nd Constitutional Amendment Act, 1976. They are enshrined under Article 51A in Part IV-A.
*   **Inspiration:** The incorporation was inspired by the Constitution of the erstwhile USSR (now Russia).
*   **Nature:** These constitute moral and civic obligations. They are non-justiciable (not directly enforceable by courts), though Parliament may legislate for their enforcement.
*   **Genesis:** The duties were introduced following recommendations by a government-appointed high-level committee during the internal emergency.

### Landmark Facts/Cases
*   **The Swaran Singh Committee:** This committee was tasked with evaluating the necessity of Fundamental Duties.
*   **Legislative Evolution:**
    *   **Original Count:** Ten duties were added in 1976 based on the Swaran Singh Committee recommendations.
    *   **Current Count:** Eleven duties exist, following the addition of the 11th duty via the 86th Amendment Act, 2002.
*   **Judicial Interpretation:** Per *M.C. Mehta v. Union of India (1983)*, the Supreme Court held that Fundamental Duties act as a "yardstick" for interpreting other constitutional provisions and evaluating the validity of laws.

### Trap Analysis
*   **The "Committee" Trap:** Do not conflate the **Swaran Singh Committee** (Fundamental Duties) with other historical committees like the Nehru Committee (1928), Gadgil Committee, or the Mandal Commission.
*   **The "Enforceability" Myth:** Fundamental Duties are **non-justiciable**, unlike Fundamental Rights, which are justiciable.
*   **The "Original Text" Trap:** Fundamental Duties were **not** in the original Constitution; they were added 26 years later in 1976.
*   **The "Count" Trap:** Always distinguish between the original count (10) and the current count (11, added in 2002).
