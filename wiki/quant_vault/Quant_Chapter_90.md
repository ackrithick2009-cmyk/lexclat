# Quant Chapter 90: Mixed Arithmetic & Averages

## Concept Primer
This module focuses on three core pillars of competitive quantitative aptitude:
1. **Ratio & Proportion Management:** Establishing relationships between multiple variables (spending/age) and scaling them to common denominators.
2. **Weighted Averaging & Correction:** Manipulating data sets where individual entries change or are corrected, requiring an understanding of "sum of observations."
3. **Logical Arithmetic:** Application of percentage changes, profit calculations, and identifying numerical properties (rationality/divisibility).

## Landmark Facts (Key Problems & Methodologies)
*   **Average Correction Protocol (Q140):** When entries are recorded incorrectly, use the **Net Deviation** method:
    *   *Correction = (New Value - Old Value).*
    *   *Net Change in Average = (Total Net Deviation) / (Total Number of Observations).*
*   **Ratio Normalization (Q131):** Normalize spending to a common base to establish the final triple ratio.
*   **The "Profit/Loss" Pivot (Q132):** Define the "original price" as $100$. Calculate the cost price relative to the initial variable, then compare the new selling price to determine the gain/loss percentage.
*   **Age Dynamics (Q133):** Define present ages using a common variable (e.g., $5x$ and $3x$). Set up algebraic equations for specific time shifts (past/future) to solve for $x$.
*   **Number Theory (Q135 & Q137):** 
    *   For division remainders: Subtract the remainders from the dividends first, then calculate the HCF.
    *   Prime vs. Composite: Prime numbers have only 1 and itself as factors; composite numbers have at least one additional factor.

## Trap Analysis (Exam Pitfalls)
*   **The "Rationality" Trap (Q134):** A quotient of two integers is rational, but $\sqrt{2}/\sqrt{3}$ is irrational because it involves the square root of a non-perfect square.
*   **Sequential Spending Error (Q139):** When spending in stages (e.g., half, then half of the *remainder*), do not divide the total by the number of stages. **Correct approach:** Work backward from the final balance.
*   **Correction Sign Reversal (Q140):** Always use **(Actual - Wrong)** to determine the sign. If 56 was entered as 42, the sum lost 14; if 32 was entered as 74, the sum gained 42.
*   **HCF Assumption (Q135):** Always perform $(X-a)$ and $(Y-b)$ *before* computing the HCF. Failing to subtract the remainder first is a critical error.
