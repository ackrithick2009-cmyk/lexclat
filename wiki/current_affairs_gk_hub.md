## 1. Legal Current Affairs (The New Criminal Laws 2024-2025)
> *CLAT relies heavily on the intersection of Legal Reasoning and Current Affairs.*

On July 1, 2024, India replaced its colonial-era criminal laws with three new codes. These are the **most critical** topics for CLAT 2026.

### Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) - Replaces IPC
- **Key Changes**: 
  - **Sedition (Sec 124A IPC)** is repealed but replaced with **Section 152 BNS** (Acts endangering sovereignty, unity, and integrity of India). 
  - **Mob Lynching** is now a distinct offense (Section 103(2)) punishable by life imprisonment or death.
  - **Terrorism** is defined for the first time in general criminal law (Section 113).
  - **Snatching** is defined as a new offense (Section 304).
  - **Community Service** is officially introduced as a form of punishment for petty offenses like defamation or public intoxication.

### Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS) - Replaces CrPC
- **Key Changes**:
  - **Zero FIR**: Can be filed anywhere, regardless of jurisdiction. Must be transferred to the relevant police station within 15 days.
  - **Electronic FIR (e-FIR)**: Introduced, but requires physical signature within 3 days.
  - **Police Custody**: Can be sought in parts extending up to 60 or 90 days (previously capped at 15 days), raising concerns about civil liberties.
  - **Forensics**: Mandatory forensic investigation for offenses punishable by 7+ years.

### Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA) - Replaces Evidence Act
- **Key Changes**:
  - **Electronic Evidence**: Treated exactly the same as primary paper evidence.
  - **Secondary Evidence**: Scope expanded to include oral admissions and written admissions.

## 2. Landmark Supreme Court Judgments (2025)

| Case Name | Subject Matter | Key Ruling |
|-----------|----------------|------------|
| *AMU Minority Status Case* | Article 30 | SC ruled that Aligarh Muslim University's minority status must be decided by a regular bench based on the *Basha* judgment parameters. Overruled the 1967 decision. |
| *Electoral Bonds Case* | Article 19(1)(a) | Declared the Electoral Bonds scheme unconstitutional. Struck down amendments to the Companies Act and RPA, citing violation of voters' right to information. |
| *Sub-classification of SC/STs* | Article 14 | States CAN sub-classify Scheduled Castes to provide separate quotas for more marginalized communities within the SC list. Overruled *E.V. Chinnaiah*. |
| *Child Marriage Act vs Personal Law* | Secular Laws | Prohibition of Child Marriage Act (PCMA) overrides personal laws (like Muslim Personal Law). Child marriage violates Article 21. |

### India-EU Free Trade Agreement (FTA)
- Negotiations resumed in 2022 after a decade; **substantive rounds ongoing in 2025**
- Key areas: **tariff reductions on automobiles, wines, dairy** (EU demands) vs. **skilled professional mobility, IT services access** (India demands)
- **TRIPS+ IP provisions**: Key sticking point — EU wants stronger pharma patent protections
- *CLAT Angle*: Questions on trade sovereignty, WTO framework, MFN clause

### United Nations Reforms
- India's persistent demand for **UNSC permanent seat** (P5 expansion)
- Reform of the **Security Council**: "Uniting for Consensus" group (Italy, Pakistan, South Korea)
- **UN Pact for the Future** adopted at UNGA September 2024 — AI governance, digital public infrastructure

## 4. Constitutional & Historical Milestones

### 75 Years of the Indian Constitution (2024–2025)

- Constitution adopted: **26 November 1949** (Constitution Day / Samvidhan Divas)
- Came into force: **26 January 1950**
- **75th Amendment Year** celebrations ran throughout 2024-25
- Key committees: Dr. B.R. Ambedkar (Drafting Committee Chairman), Rajendra Prasad (President, Constituent Assembly)

### Important Constitutional Amendments (CLAT Frequently Tested)

| Amendment | Year | Key Change |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| **1st Amendment** | 1951 | Added 9th Schedule (land reform laws beyond judicial review) |
| **42nd Amendment** | 1976 | Added "Socialist, Secular" to Preamble; Emergency-era changes |
| **44th Amendment** | 1978 | Restored right to property as legal right (Art. 300A); removed Art. 19(1)(f) |
| **73rd & 74th Amendment** | 1992 | Panchayati Raj and Municipalities — constitutional status |
| **86th Amendment** | 2002 | Art. 21A — Free & Compulsory Education (6-14 years) |
| **101st Amendment** | 2016 | GST — subsumed multiple indirect taxes |
| **103rd Amendment** | 2019 | 10% EWS reservation in education and government jobs |
