| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Questions** | 10–14 |

> ⚠️ **CLAT Reality**: You do NOT need advanced math. You need **speed and approximation**. Most answers can be found by eliminating 2–3 wrong options without exact calculation.

## The 5 DI Formats (Ranked by Frequency)

### 1. Table DI (Most Common)

**Structure**: A data table with multiple columns and rows. Questions ask for ratios, percentages, differences, and rankings.

**Speed Strategy**:
- **Read the table headers first** — understand what each column represents
- **Note the units** — lakhs, crores, percentages, thousands
- **Look for patterns** — which rows/columns are highest/lowest
- **Skip exact calculation** — estimate and pick the closest option

**Example Table**:

| Company | Revenue (₹ Cr) | Profit (₹ Cr) | Employees |
|---------|----------------|---------------|-----------|
| A | 500 | 50 | 1000 |
| B | 800 | 120 | 2000 |
| C | 600 | 90 | 1500 |

**Question**: Which company has the highest profit per employee?

**Exact Calculation**:
- A: 50/1000 = 0.05 Cr/employee
- B: 120/2000 = 0.06 Cr/employee
- C: 90/1500 = 0.06 Cr/employee

**Approximation Trick**:
- A: 50/1000 = 1/20 = 0.05
- B: 120/2000 = 12/200 = 6/100 = 0.06
- C: 90/1500 = 9/150 = 6/100 = 0.06

> **Answer**: B and C tie. But B has higher absolute profit — the question asks for highest profit per employee, so B and C are equal. Wait — recalculate: C = 90/1500 = 0.06 exactly. B = 120/2000 = 0.06 exactly. **Tie!** The answer would be "Both B and C" or a more precise option.

> **Key Rule**: Always check if the question asks for exact ranking or approximate. If exact, calculate precisely. If approximate, estimate.

---

### 2. Bar Graph DI

**Structure**: Bars representing values across categories. Questions ask for comparisons, growth rates, and ratios.

**Speed Strategy**:
- **Visually compare bar heights** before calculating
- **Use the grid lines** to estimate values
- **Growth rate = (New - Old) / Old × 100** — memorize this formula

**Example**: Bar graph shows exports of 5 countries.

**Question**: Which country showed the highest percentage growth in exports from 2023 to 2024?

**Trap**: The country with the highest absolute increase may NOT have the highest percentage growth. A country going from 10 to 20 has 100% growth, while one going from 100 to 150 has only 50% growth.

> **Key Rule**: Percentage growth = (Increase / Original) × 100. Always use the original value as the denominator.

---

### 3. Pie Chart DI

**Structure**: A circle divided into sectors representing percentages of a whole.

**Speed Strategy**:
- **Convert percentages to actual values FIRST** — if total is 1000 Cr and a sector is 25%, the value is 250 Cr
- **Use the angle** — 100% = 360°, so 1% = 3.6°. If you know the angle, you can calculate the percentage
- **Compare sectors visually** — the largest sector is obvious without calculation

**Example**: Pie chart showing market share of 5 companies. Total market = 2000 Cr.

**Question**: What is the difference in market share between Company A (30%) and Company B (20%)?

**Calculation**:
- A: 30% of 2000 = 600 Cr
- B: 20% of 2000 = 400 Cr
- Difference: 200 Cr

> **Shortcut**: 30% - 20% = 10% of 2000 = 200 Cr. Calculate the percentage difference first, then apply to total.

---

### 4. Line Graph DI

**Structure**: A line showing trends over time. Questions ask for growth rates, rate of change, and comparative analysis.

**Speed Strategy**:
- **Look at the slope** — steeper slope = faster growth
- **Identify peaks and troughs** — these are frequently tested
- **Calculate average growth rate** over a period

**Example**: Line graph shows GDP growth from 2019 to 2024.

**Question**: What was the average annual GDP growth from 2020 to 2024?

**Calculation**:
- Values: -5% (2020), 3% (2021), 7% (2022), 6% (2023), 8% (2024)
- Average = (-5 + 3 + 7 + 6 + 8) / 5 = 19 / 5 = 3.8%

> **Trap**: Do NOT calculate the total growth from 2020 to 2024 and divide by 5. You must average the annual growth rates, not the cumulative growth.

---

### 5. Caselet DI (CLAT's Favorite)

**Structure**: A 200-word paragraph with embedded numerical data. You must extract data, organize it, and answer questions.

**Speed Strategy**:
- **Read the paragraph once** to understand the story
- **Extract data into a table** as you read — don't rely on memory
- **Identify what is given and what is asked** — the question may require a multi-step calculation

**Example Caselet**:
> "A company has three departments: Sales, Marketing, and HR. The total number of employees is 600. Sales has 40% of the employees. Marketing has 25% more employees than HR. The average salary in Sales is ₹50,000, in Marketing is ₹60,000, and in HR is ₹40,000."

**Extracted Table**:

| Department | % of Total | Employees | Avg Salary |
|------------|------------|-----------|------------|
| Sales | 40% | 240 | ₹50,000 |
| Marketing | 35% | 210 | ₹60,000 |
| HR | 25% | 150 | ₹40,000 |

*(Note: Marketing = HR + 25% of HR = 1.25 × HR. Let HR = x. Then x + 1.25x = 360 (remaining 60%). 2.25x = 360. x = 160. So HR = 160, Marketing = 200. Wait — let me recalculate. Total = 600. Sales = 40% = 240. Remaining = 360. Marketing = 1.25 × HR. So HR + 1.25HR = 360. 2.25HR = 360. HR = 160. Marketing = 200.)*

**Question**: What is the total salary expenditure of the company?

**Calculation**:
- Sales: 240 × 50,000 = 12,000,000
- Marketing: 200 × 60,000 = 12,000,000
- HR: 160 × 40,000 = 6,400,000
- **Total: 30,400,000** = ₹3.04 Cr

> **Key Rule**: Caselet DI is the most time-consuming but also the most scoring if you extract data correctly. Spend 30 seconds creating a table — it will save 2 minutes on calculations.

## The Fraction-Percentage Grid (Memorize Cold)

Convert percentages to fractions instantly:

| Fraction | Percentage | Decimal |
|----------|------------|---------|
| 1/2 | 50% | 0.5 |
| 1/3 | 33.33% | 0.333 |
| 1/4 | 25% | 0.25 |
| 1/5 | 20% | 0.2 |
| 1/6 | 16.66% | 0.166 |
| 1/8 | 12.5% | 0.125 |
| 1/10 | 10% | 0.1 |
| 1/12 | 8.33% | 0.083 |
| 2/3 | 66.66% | 0.666 |
| 3/4 | 75% | 0.75 |
| 3/5 | 60% | 0.6 |
| 4/5 | 80% | 0.8 |

> **CLAT Strategy**: If a question asks for 37.5% of a number, recognize that 37.5% = 3/8. So 37.5% of 800 = 3/8 × 800 = 300. No calculator needed.

## The Approximation Rule

**When to Approximate**:
- The options are far apart (e.g., 20%, 35%, 50%, 65%)
- The question asks for "approximately" or "closest to"
- You are running out of time

**When to Calculate Exactly**:
- The options are close together (e.g., 24%, 25%, 26%, 27%)
- The question asks for "exactly" or "precisely"
- It is a ranking question (highest/lowest)

**Approximation Examples**:

| Exact | Approximate | When to Use |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| 49/100 | 50/100 = 1/2 | Options are far apart |
| 101/200 | 100/200 = 1/2 | Quick estimate |
| 24/75 | 24/72 = 1/3 | Slightly adjust denominator for easy division |
| 38/95 | 38/95 ≈ 40/100 = 0.4 | Round numerator and denominator |
