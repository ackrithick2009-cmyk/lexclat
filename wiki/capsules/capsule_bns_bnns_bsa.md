## The Exact Trigger (Memorize Cold)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| **December 25, 2023** | Presidential Assent received |
| **July 1, 2024** | Officially came into force across India |
| **Exception** | BNS Section 106(2) (hit-and-run) held back due to transport strikes |

> ⚠️ **CLAT Trap**: If a passage says "all provisions came into force on July 1, 2024," that is **FALSE** — Section 106(2) was deferred.

## The Structural Shift: Colonial → Modern

**Main Contextual Influence**: The overarching driver was a structural shift from colonial-era retributive policing to modern, technology-driven restorative justice. The IPC (1860), CrPC (1973), and Evidence Act (1872) were fundamentally unequipped to handle digital forensics, cloud-stored data, or decentralized organized crime networks.

---

## Core Legal Underpinnings

### 1. Decolonial Jurisprudence

Replacing **Sedition (Old IPC Sec 124A)** with **Acts Endangering Sovereignty (BNS Sec 152)** to remove the colonial construct of criminalizing mere disaffection toward the government, shifting the focus strictly to threats against the state's integrity.

| | Old IPC (1860) | New BNS (2023) |
|--|----------------|----------------|
| **Sedition** | Sec 124A — criminalized "disaffection" toward government | **REPEALED** |
| **Sovereignty** | Not a standalone offense | **Sec 152** — acts endangering sovereignty, unity, integrity |
| **Key Difference** | "Disaffection" = criticizing the government | "Endangering sovereignty" = actual threats to territorial integrity |

> **Why this matters for CLAT**: The passage will likely present a scenario where someone criticizes the government and asks whether Sec 152 applies. The answer is **NO** — mere criticism is not "endangering sovereignty."

### 2. Evidentiary Modernization

The BSA updates **Section 57** to recognize electronic records as **primary evidence**. This directly integrates the judicial framework established in **Anvar P.V. v. P.K. Basheer (2014)** regarding certificate requirements for electronic data.

> ⚠️ **Key Reasoning Trap**: BSA makes electronic records **primary evidence** (not secondary). Examiners love flipping this in options.

## CLAT Passage Strategy

### The Three Things You Must Know

1. **The exact trigger** (Dec 25, 2023 → July 1, 2024)
2. **The constitutional/legal provision at stake** (Art. 21, Art. 14, decolonial jurisprudence)
3. **The competing interest that was overridden and why** (colonial sedition law → modern sovereignty test; privacy concerns → evidentiary necessity)

### How CLAT Will Test This

- When a passage mentions "electronic evidence" or "digital forensics," look for references to **Anvar P.V.** and the BSA's evidentiary modernization.
- When a passage discusses "sedition" or "disaffection," the answer is likely about **BNS Section 152** and the shift from colonial "sedition" to "endangering sovereignty."
- The BNS reduced the number of offenses from **511 to 358**. This decriminalization trend is a key theme.

### Proportionality Connection

If a question asks about the **least restrictive means** to achieve a legitimate aim, the old sedition law (criminalizing mere disaffection) was **not** the least restrictive means. The new BNS Section 152 is more narrowly tailored.
